Intel Sustainability Goals

Explore Intel's journey and 2030+ sustainability goals on a horizontal timeline.



1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, setting the stage for decades of innovation.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor — a milestone for computing and energy efficiency trends.

1979

8086 Processor

Launch of the 8086 processor establishes x86 architecture that powers many modern systems.

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduces the 386 processor, boosting performance and enabling complex tasks.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

Intel records its highest operations emissions and begins major investments in abatement and energy efficiency.

2020

RISE Strategy

Launch of RISE strategy and 2030 goals targeting climate action, water stewardship, and waste reduction.

2022

Net-Zero By 2040

Intel commits to net-zero (Scope 1 & 2) across global operations by 2040.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts a global Sustainability Summit to accelerate supplier and industry collaboration.
